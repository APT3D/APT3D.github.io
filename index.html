Abstract

Generating 3D houses from text has relied on resource-intensive large language models (LLMs), limiting real-time game deployment. This paper introduces Architect GPT (APT), a novel real-time text-to-house framework utilizing a small language model (sLM). APT features a modular pipeline with robust floor plan generation, sLM-based asset placement planning, and efficient asset retrieval and placement. We effectively transfer LLM spatial reasoning to sLMs by finetuning with high-quality synthetic data and by progressively transferring these capabilities to the smallest models through knowledge distillation. Our experimental results show that APT generates 3D houses ~20 times faster than LLM-based baselines, achieving comparable quality. Integrated directly into game environments on consumer GPUs, APT proves highly practical for real-time applications.

Real-Time Language Guided 3D House Generation Results

APT generates 3D houses from text-based user input in real-time, achieving high-quality results comparable to LLM-based methods. The generated scenes can be directly integrated into real-time game environments, demonstrating the practical applicability of APT for real-time applications.

Main Pipeline

APT has three main components: (1) floor plan generation algorithm, (2) object selection and constraint using the designer agent, (3) 3D asset retrieval and placement. Qualitative Comparisons Between Holodeck and APT

Qualitative comparison between Holodeck and APT. Our baseline Holodeck often suffers from unrealistic floor plans (e.g. empty spaces at the center, overly narrow rooms, and unrealistic room connections) as well as inaccurate object retrieval, resulting in outputs that deviate from real-world houses. In contrast, our model generates more realistic house by producing human-agnostic floor plans and appropriately placed objects.

Effects of Finetuning on Small Language Models

Qualitative comparison between our finetuned model (APT) and the base Qwen-2.5 0.5B model across a diverse set of user prompts.
